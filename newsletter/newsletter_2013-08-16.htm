GRADED STAKES RACE PREVIEWS, ENTRIES & ML ODDS

Alabama Stakes at Saratoga Race Course (Grade 1): Princess Of Sylmar goes up against Fiftyshadesofhay - Todd Pletcher pupil has won six of her last seven starts (four of five this year) - a second in the Grade 2 Gazelle Stakes to Close Hatches at Aqueduct April 6 being the only blemish in the last 10 months. The wins include the Grade 1 American Oaks last time out July 20 at Saratoga, the Grade 1 Kentucky Oaks at 38-1 odds over Dreaming of Julia, the minor Busher Stakes at Aqueduct February 2 to follow another win at Aqueduct - the Busanda Stakes by 7 1/2 lengths January 4. She also has a MSW and an Allowance Optional Claiming score. She is absolutely on fire and has two straight Grade 1 wins under her belt ahead of this weekend. She likely need just this win to secure the Eclipse Award and obviously is the one to beat! She breaks from post 4 with Javier Castellano in the saddle. Read More

Sword Dancer Invitational Stakes at Saratoga Race Course (Grade 1): The Sword Dancer has attracted a large field of 13 for race 9 on Saratoga's Saturday card. Post time is 5:12 PM. Let's take a look into the past performances of all 13 combatants for this lucrative Grade 1 stakes. Boisterous - has won seven of his last 13 starts for trainer Shug McGaughey including the 2011 and 2012 Grade 3 Red Smith Stakes at Aqueduct and the 2011 and 2012 Knickerbocker Stakes also at Aqueduct. His lone poor performance the last couple years came in August 2012 - a ninth in the Grade 1 Arlington Millions but he won two straight after that. He had a pair of good seconds recently - in the Grade 3 Ben Ali Stakes last time out at Keeneland April 21 after the Grade 3 River City Handicap at Churchill Downs November 5 but has taken his game to another level in his last two starts - wins in the Grade 2 Monmouth Stakes June 9 and in the Grade 1 Man o' War Stakes July 13 at Belmont Park. He now has four wins and two seconds in his last six starts overall and is obviously in the best form of his career. Boisterous is on fire, will have John Velazquez in the saddle, breaks from post 11, is the 123 pound co-highweight and is the horse to beat at 5-2 odds Saturday. Read More

2013 Del Mar Oaks at Del Mar (Grade 1): Wishing Gate - Thomas Proctor trained filly has three wins and two seconds in eight career starts but is coming off a terrific result - a win in her stakes debut in the Grade 2 San Clemente at 5-1 odds at Del Mar July 21. She also had a win in an Allowance Optional Claiming at Arlington Park June 23 after a second in an Allowance in May. Other notable career results include a second in the minor Surfer Girl Stakes at Santa Anita in October after an eighth in the Oak Tree Juvenile Fillies Turf in September. Traffic could be an issue for her stalking style but she has won two of three starts career on this track already and is the one to beat Saturday. Read More

Lake Placid Stakes at Saratoga Race Course (Grade 2): Tapicat - legendary Bill Mott trained Tapicat has won three of her five career starts - three in a row including the Grade 3 Florida Oaks at Tampa Bay Downs way back on February 2, a one mile Allowance Optional Claiming score by 5 1/4 January 9 at Gulfstream and a MSW score before that. Last time out was one of her two blemishes - an OK third in the Grade 2 Lake George Stakes here July 24 after 5 1/2 months away. Her other career start was a third in a MSW at Belmont Park October 28. She is 9-5 on the morning line and breaks from post 2 Sunday. Read More

Philip H. Iselin Stakes at Monmouth Park (Grade 3): Last Gunfighter - Chad Brown trained colt had a six race win streak snapped last time out with a respectable second in the Grade 2 Suburban Handicap behind Flat Out at Belmont Park July 7 with a career best 103 Beyer Speed Figure. The wins include two minor stakes scores - the Evening Attire at Aqueduct January 19 over Isn't He Perfect and the Raymond Earl Stakes by 4 3/4 lengths December 9 also at Aqueduct. He also won the Grade 3 Pimlico Special May 17 by an impressive 4 1/2 lengths after a win in his graded stakes debut - the Grade 3 Excelsior Stakes at Aqueduct March 23. He has placed in all 11 career starts - those six wins, three seconds and two third place finishes and is one of the hottest horses in the land ahead of Sunday's action. He is the unquestioned favorite and the one everyone will be chasing in the Philip Iselin. Read More
