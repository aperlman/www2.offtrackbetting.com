GRADED STAKES RACE PREVIEWS & RACE FAVORITES

2013 Charles Town Classic Stakes: - (Grade 2): Game on Dude - Bob Baffert trained gelding is a multiple Grade 1 winner. He has six graded stakes scores in his last nine starts & was a consideration for last year's Eclipse Award for older male and if not for Wise Dan, he may have been in the running this year again.

Ron the Greek - looked very good in 2012 with two wins and three second place finishes in five starts before a sixth in the Jockey Club Gold Cup at Belmont Park September 29 and an OK fourth in the Breeders' Cup Classic in November. Read More

Illinois Derby at Hawthorne Race Course (Grade 3): Departing - has four career starts for trainer Albert Stall Jr. and three wins - the last and most impressive being at the minor Texas Heritage Stakes at Sam Houston March 2. Before that was an Allowance Optional Claiming score here in January and a MSW score in her career debut December 22. Last time out was a good third in the Grade 2 Louisiana Derby March 30 at Fair Grounds which was enough to make him the morning line favorite.

Siete de Oros - has seven career starts with two wins - a Maiden Claiming and an Allowance at Parx. He had a second by a head in the Grade 2 Jerome Stakes at 41-1 odds at Aqueduct January 5 behind Vyjack and had two third place finishes recently - two back in the Grade 3 Withers and in the minor Pennsylvania Nursery Stakes at Parx December 8. Last time out was a fourth place finish in the Grade 3 Gotham Stakes at Aqueduct March 2. He has been at or near the top in every one of his seven career starts and is certainly a threat in the Illinois Derby. Read More

Coolmore Lexington Stakes at Keeneland Race Course (Grade 3): Pure Fun - Kenneth McPeek trained filly is coming off an OK third in the Grade 3 Fathead Bourbonette March 23 after a terrific win in the Grade 1 Hollywood Starlet Stakes at Hollywood Park December 8 and an Allowance Optional Claiming score at Churchill Downs in November by 9 1/4 lengths. In her other six career starts she managed a win, a second and a third place finish. She has been impressive the last six months and should give the boys a good run for their money on Saturday as she tries to gather points for the Kentucky Oaks, not the Derby - she needs a win to qualify.

Cerro - Irish bred son of Mr. Greeley and H. Graham Motion trainee Cerro has six career starts with two wins - in his debut at Milan and last time out in an Allowance Optional Claiming here January 26 after a third and a fourth respectively in similar races. His North American debut was a sixth in the Grade 3 Pilgrim Stakes at Belmont Park in June. He is coming off a sixth in the Grade 2 Fountain of Youth Stakes February 23 after an Allowance Optional Claiming score January 26 at Gulfstream. He needs a win here for a possible spot in the Preakness Stakes May 18 - the Kentucky Derby is already out of reach. Read More
