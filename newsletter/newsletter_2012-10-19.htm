GRADED STAKES RACE PREVIEWS & FAVORITES

SENATOR KEN MADDY STAKES at Santa Anita (Grade 3): Broken Dreams - is the defending champion of this race but comes into this year's edition is so-so form. She has placed in just one of five starts - a win two back in the minor Osunitas Stakes at Del Mar July 21 after a fourth in the Redondo Beach Stakes and a fifth in the Great Lady M Stakes.

Byrama - has been decent this year with three wins, three seconds and a third place finish in nine starts. Last time out was a win in the minor Unzip Me Stakes here September 29 to follow a second in the C.E.R.F Stakes September 5. Read More

RAVEN RUN STAKES at Keeneland (Grade 2): Gypsy Robin - had won three straight starts before a sixth in the Grade 3 Victory Ride at Belmont on July 7 and a good second to Contested last time out in the Grade 1 Test Stakes at Saratoga August 25. Her wins during the streak included the Grade 2 Beaumont Stakes at here on March 15, the Inaugural Stakes at Presque Isle Downs on May 15 and the Sorority Stakes at Monmouth Park on September 5 of 2011. She has won both of her career starts on this track and enters in decent form.

Killer Graces - Killer Graces surprisingly has only three career wins. Lately it has been a bit of a struggle for the Jerry Hollendorfer filly - two fourths and two fifths in her last four starts including a fourth behind the likes of Eden's Moon in the Grade 2 Hollywood Oaks June 23. Read More

LADIES' SHAPING UP AS A TRUE CLASSIC at Santa Anita (Breeders' Cup Preview): While the Classic is sure to bring a rousing end to the weekend, the most highly anticipated Breeders' Cup race could take place Friday with the $2 million Ladies' Classic. Run at 1 1/8 miles, the Ladies' Classic is shaping up as one of the best renewals ever, with a championship surely lying in the balance. Read More

2012 Breeders' Cup Race Fields Will Be Announced on Wednesday October 24th: While the older horses have taken turns beating each other throughout the year they represent a formidable and competitive group that should make the 1 1/4-mile Classic the signature event of the weekend. Favoritism, due to both talent and a potential home field advantage, could go to Diamond Pride, Lanni Family Trust, Mercedes Stable and Bernie Schiappa's GAME ON DUDE, who will look to better his second-place finish in last year's Classic at Churchill Downs. The 5-year-old son of 1998 Classic winner Awesome Again has been a gem of consistency all season for trainer Bob Baffert, registering four wins from six starts and will head to post with a 9-for-20 career record.
