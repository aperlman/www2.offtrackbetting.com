GRADED STAKES RACE PREVIEWS & FAVORITES

Bluegrass Stakes 2014 (G1) at Keeneland, Saturday, April 12 : The "Road to the Kentucky Derby" makes a stop at Keeneland Race Course in Lexington, Kentucky this Saturday, April 12. The Toyota Blue Grass Stakes will offer up one of the final chances for three year olds to gather points toward qualification into the Run for the Roses in less than a month's time. The hopefuls will go 1 1/8 miles on Keeneland's main track for a very nice purse of $750,000.

Bobby's Kitten - Chad Brown trained colt broke maiden August 23 in wire-to-wire fashion in his second career start at Saratoga after a third in his debut July 27 also at Saratoga. He went on to an impressive 6 1/4 length score in the Grade 2 Pilgrim Stakes at Belmont Park October 6 and finished a decent third in the Breeders' Cup Juvenile Turf. Last time out was another win - a one mile Allowance score in gate-to-wire fashion at Tampa Bay Downs March 8 making it three wins and two third place finishes in five career starts. He makes his first start on Polytrack and his fist start off grass as the favorite Saturday. Javier Castellano gets the mount from post 5. Read More

Medal Count - Dale Romans trained colt is coming off a win just last week in the Grade 3 Transylvania Stakes on this track April 4 after a fifth in the Grade 2 Fountain of Youth Stakes at Gulfstream February 22, an Allowance Optional Claiming score January 12 and an eleventh in the Breeders' Cup Juvenile. That followed an equally disappointing fifth in the Grade 3 Bourbon Stakes at Keeneland in October and a MSW score in his career debut - at Ellis Park September 1. Medal Count hadn't been great in his graded stakes action but his effort on this track last week certainly opened some eyes. He is the second choice on the morning line. Robby Alvarado will be in the saddle from gate 13. Read More
