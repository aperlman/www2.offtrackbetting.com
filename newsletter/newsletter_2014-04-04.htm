GRADED STAKES RACE PREVIEWS & FAVORITES

Wood Memorial 2014 (G1) at Aqueduct, Saturday, April 5 : Undefeated New York-bred Samraat looks to head to the Kentucky Derby with his perfect record intact. He's expected to face untested Social Inclusion, fresh off of a romping Gulfstream Park win, archrival Uncle Sigh, and more at Aqueduct Racetrack on a nationally televised broadcast. The Grade 1, $1 million Wood Memorial offers a total of 170 points, 100 to the winner, on the Road to the Kentucky Derby. The tremendous card also features the Grade 1 Carter Handicap, Grade 2 Gazelle Stakes, and Grade 3 Bay Shore.

Social Inclusion - highly regarded Manuel Azpurua trained colt makes his stakes debut Saturday having won both of his career starts. Last time out was an Allowance score over Remsen winner Honor Code in track record time at Gulfstream March 12 - by 10 lengths after breaking maiden February 22 also on that track. He has the highest Beyer Speed Figure (111) of any 3-year-old racing so far this season. Despite the lack of experience Social Inclusion is the 2-1 morning line favorite for the Wood Memorial. He leaves from the far outside post with Luis Contreras in the saddle. Read More

Samraat - Richard Violette trained New York bred colt will try to remain perfect in his career. He has four starts and four wins - last time out over Uncle Sigh in the Grade 3 Withers Stakes here February 1, before that in his stakes debut - the listed Damon Runyon Stakes by an astounding 16 3/4 lengths here December 18, an Allowance score on this track November 22 and a MSW by 3 1/2 lengths score in his debut at Belmont Park in October. Samraat is the 7-2 second choice on the morning line and will leave from gate 8 with Jose Ortiz aboard. Read More

Santa Anita Derby 2014 (G1) at Santa Anita Park, Saturday, April 5 California-bred sensation California Chrome heads a field of eight sophomores set to go 1 1/8 miles in Saturday's Grade I, $1 million Santa Anita Derby, as the Art Sherman trainee seeks his fourth consecutive win and 100 additional Kentucky Derby qualifying points. The second, third and fourth place finishers will each receive 40, 20 and 10 qualifying points respectively. Read More

Candy Boy - highly regarded colt has six career starts for trainer John Sadler - a pair of fourths to start his career to go along with a pair of seconds including the Grade 1 CashCall Futurity behind Eclipse Award winner Shared Belief December 14 with a 95 Beyer Speed Figure. Last time out Candy Boy took his game to a different level with a terrific win over Chitu (with a 96 Beyer Speed Figure) in the Grade 2 Robert B. Lewis Stakes on this track February 8. He has two wins and two seconds in his last four starts and like California Chrome is peaking at just the right time. He leaves from gate 6 Saturday with the legendary Gary Stevens in the saddle as the 9-5 second choice. Read More

Santa Anita Oaks 2014 (G1) at Santa Anita Park, Saturday, April 5: Fashion Plate - Simon Callahan trainee broke maiden December 29 in gate to wire fashion by 6 1/2 lengths in her third career start - at Santa Anita December 29 after a third and a seventh respectively in a pair of MSW races. She made a huge statement last time out with a terrific win over Streaming in the Grade 1 Las Virgenes Stakes with a career best 83 Beyer Speed Figure on this track February 1. Fashion Plate starts from the rail with Hall of Fame jockey Gary Stevens aboard as the likely favorite Saturday. Read More

Arethusa - is coming off a third behind Fashion Plate in the Grade 1 Las Virgenes Stakes here February 1 after a fifth in her graded stakes debut - the Grade 1 Hollywood Starlet December 7 at Hollywood Park and a win her stakes debut - the Sharp Cat Stakes on that same track November 16. Prior to those results were three MSW races which produced a second and a third place finish. She has a win, a second and two third place finishes in six career starts and should provide the field a good test Saturday. Rafael Bejarano gets the call from gate 3. Read More
